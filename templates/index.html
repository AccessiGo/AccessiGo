Why AccessiGo

Plan the entrance, not just the destination.

Traditional campus maps can get someone to a building, but they rarely explain which door is usable, where the ramp starts, or whether an entrance photo is current. AccessiGo turns those details into a clearer accessibility layer.

  • Accessible entrances shown clearly AccessiGo focuses on the part of a trip that campus maps often skip: which entrance is actually wheelchair-accessible.
  • Built around real campus movement The first version centers on outdoor accessibility at the University of Windsor so routes can be planned between buildings, parking, sidewalks, ramps, and doors.
  • Photos help keep access data current Community-sourced entrance photos can be uploaded and reviewed with a quick accessibility signal as buildings, pathways, and temporary barriers change.
  • Designed to scale past one campus The same workflow can expand to other schools with local map overlays, entrance details, verified photos, and accessibility metadata.
Outdoor accessibility first

Start with entrances, sidewalks, ramps, and exterior routes. Then expand indoors once the most visible access barriers are documented.
